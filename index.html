Welcome to GNOSIS

Here at GNOSIS, we say it’s time to question out-of-context interpretations, contradictory verses, and doomsday predictions that never seem to pan out. (Looking at you, 1844, 1988, 2011...)

Armed with a Master’s in Theology (and a penchant for snark), we’re here to guide you through the glorious maze of biblical passages—helping you see them for their symbolic, poetic, and metaphorical brilliance rather than reading them like a boring, overly literal instruction manual.

Contradictions & Absurdities

Because reading ancient texts literally—word for word—can lead to head-scratching moments like these:

1. God Incites or Satan Incites?

2 Samuel 24:1 vs. 1 Chronicles 21:1

The Conflict

In 2 Samuel 24:1, we see God supposedly incite David to take a census. But in 1 Chronicles 21:1, Satan does the inciting. Wait... so which is it? Did the authors forget to compare notes? Or maybe there’s a deeper, non-literal meaning?

2. Noah’s Ark: Two or Seven?

Genesis 6:19-20 vs. Genesis 7:2-3

The Conflict

First, God says to bring two of each animal. Then it changes to seven pairs of each clean animal. Did we need extra spares for the post-Flood barbecue? Multiple sources stitched together, or a brilliant invitation to read beyond the literal? You decide.

3. The Death of Judas

Matthew 27:5 vs. Acts 1:18

The Conflict

Did Judas hang himself, or did he fall headlong and burst open in a field? Look, we get it—he’s not the most sympathetic figure. But if you’re taking everything literally, you’ll need some imaginative contortions to reconcile these two.

Reflections

At GNOSIS, we're not out to destroy faith—we're nudging people to see that a **strictly literal** reading can be as clunky as dial-up internet in a 5G world.

"Because if every verse is literal, we should all be worried about whale-swallowing incidents, donkey conversations, and weekly instructions to put entire cities to the sword."

By embracing context, poetry, allegory, and historical nuance, you just might find new dimensions of meaning—without stoking apocalyptic hysteria or drowning in contradictions.
